# IDEA: Our invoicing app needs to handle customers who bill the same clients every month

## Who I am
I'm the Head of Customer Success at a company that makes invoicing software for freelancers
and small agencies. Our product has been live for six years and works well — people create
an invoice, send it, get paid. I sit on the calls where customers tell us what's missing.

## The problem
The #1 thing I hear, every week: "most of my clients are on retainer — I bill them the same
amount on the 1st of every month — why am I recreating the same invoice by hand 12 times a
year?" Today they duplicate last month's invoice, change the date, and send it. They forget.
They send late. Some of them churn to a competitor specifically because we don't do this.
We're leaving our most loyal, highest-value customers doing the most tedious work.

## What I wish existed
A way to set up an invoice **once** and have it go out on a schedule — monthly, or every few
weeks — to the same client, for the same (or a similar) amount, without the customer lifting
a finger each time. They should be able to see what's scheduled, pause or skip a month, and
tweak an amount before it sends. When one goes out automatically, they should know it did.

## Who would use it
- **Existing customers** on retainer relationships — they set up a recurring invoice and
  mostly forget about it.
- **Their clients** — who just receive a normal-looking invoice on a predictable day.
- **Our support team** — who need to answer "why did/didn't this send?" without guessing.

## Constraints & must-haves
- It has to feel like part of the app people already know — not a bolted-on separate thing.
- Existing one-off invoices must keep working exactly as they do now. Don't break the
  thing 100,000 people rely on to add a feature some of them want.
- People need to trust it. A recurring invoice that silently fails to send, or sends twice,
  is worse than not having the feature — it costs our customer real money and credibility.
- Customers must always be able to see what's coming and stop it before it goes.

## What success looks like
- Within a quarter, a meaningful share of active customers have at least one recurring
  invoice set up, and the "can you please add recurring invoices" requests drop off my calls.
- Support gets a clear timeline of what was scheduled and what actually sent, so "did my
  invoice go out?" is answerable in seconds.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Taking payment automatically (auto-charging a card). Just send the invoice on schedule;
  getting paid still works the way it does today.
- Complex proration or usage-based billing. This is fixed, repeating invoices.
- Reminders/dunning for overdue recurring invoices — worth doing, but a later step.
