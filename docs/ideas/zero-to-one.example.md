# IDEA: A way for my neighborhood to share tools instead of everyone buying their own

## Who I am
I live on a block of about 40 houses. Half of us own a pressure washer, a ladder, a hedge
trimmer — stuff we each use twice a year. I'm not technical; I just organize the occasional
block party and end up being the person people text when they need to borrow something.

## The problem
Right now "sharing" is a group text with 60 people in it. Someone asks "does anyone have a
tile saw," it scrolls away, three people answer an hour later, nobody remembers who has
what. I keep a mental list of who owns the good ladder and I'm tired of being the human
database. People would happily lend things if it weren't such a hassle to ask.

## What I wish existed
A simple shared place where a neighbor can list a thing they're willing to lend, and anyone
on the block can see what's available and ask to borrow it for a few days. Think "a
lending library, but for garage stuff." When something's out on loan, everyone can see it's
taken and when it's due back, so we stop double-asking.

## Who would use it
- **Lenders** — neighbors who post an item and say yes/no to requests.
- **Borrowers** — neighbors browsing what's around and asking for it.
- **Me** — I'd love to *not* be the coordinator, but I'll help people get set up at first.

## Constraints & must-haves
- Dead simple. My neighbors range from teenagers to people in their 80s. If it needs a
  manual, it's dead on arrival.
- No new account to babysit if we can avoid it — people won't remember another password.
- It's just our block, not the whole internet. I don't want strangers seeing our stuff.
- Works on a phone. Nobody's opening a laptop to lend a ladder.

## What success looks like
- In the first month, at least 15 neighbors post at least one item, and I see real
  borrow-return cycles happen without anyone texting me.
- The next time someone needs a pressure washer, they find it and ask for it themselves,
  and I never see the message.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Money — no rentals, deposits, or payments. It's a favor economy.
- Ratings/reviews of neighbors. We know each other; let's not turn it into that.
- Anything beyond our block. Maybe later, not now.
